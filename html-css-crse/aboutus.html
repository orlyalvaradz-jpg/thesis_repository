MISSION

Our mission is to develop and implement an IoT-based smart dam monitoring and early warning system that enhances flood preparedness and dam safety. Through real-time data collection, intelligent analysis, and timely alerts, we aim to support informed decision-making, promote sustainable water management, and contribute to achieving the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals — particularly SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities) and SDG 13 (Climate Action).

THE PRINSANG MOLINO (MOLINO DAM)

Molino Dam, also known as Prinza Dam, is a historic adobe irrigation structure located along the Zapote River between Barangay San Nicolas I in Bacoor, Cavite, and Talon Dos in Las Piñas City. Built during the Spanish colonial period by the Augustinian Recollects, the dam was originally designed to irrigate farmlands in Las Piñas and Bacoor and to regulate the flow of freshwater from the river to prevent saltwater intrusion from Manila Bay.

Over time, urban development and silt accumulation have reduced the dam's effectiveness in managing water flow, contributing to flooding in nearby communities. Today, while Molino Dam stands as a heritage landmark, it also plays a critical role in local flood control and environmental management.
